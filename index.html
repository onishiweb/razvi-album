About

Razvi / Rizvi Family Frames is a participatory photography project conceptualised by cousins Adam Razvi and Samee Razvi. It brings individual and private perspectives into a collective and public realm, to explore notions of identity, family and belonging.

Participants were asked to respond to ten categories with images and words, and to provide a self-portrait. The submissions form an album of family photographs revealing similarities and differences, as well as how participants chose to represent themselves in March-April 2018.

Viewers and participants might like to consider a few key points when exploring and clicking through the pages:

  • What do you see in each photo?
  • How does the text influence your reading of the photo?
  • What does each photo reveal about the person who submitted it?
  • What does your response to other people’s photos reveal about yourself?

We welcome feedback and look forward to hearing from you.
